A young shepherd boy, bored with his job, decided to play a trick on the villagers.
He ran to the village, shouting, "Wolf! Wolf! A wolf is attacking my sheep!" The
villagers rushed to help, only to find the boy laughing at them. He played this
trick again, and the villagers, angry and frustrated, warned him not to cry wolf
when there was no wolf.
Some time later, a real wolf appeared and began to attack the sheep. The boy,
terrified, ran to the village and cried out, "Wolf! Wolf! Please help!" But this
time, no one came to his aid. They thought it was another one of his tricks. By the
time the villagers went to check on the boy, the wolf had scattered the sheep.
The Moral
The boy learned a hard lesson that day: no one trusts a liar, even when they are
telling the truth. This story teaches the importance of honesty and how broken
trust is not easily regained.
